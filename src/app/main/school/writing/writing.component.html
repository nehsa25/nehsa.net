Writing / Grammar

In the search to become a better communicator..

  • One space, not two when starting new sentence. Bah!
  • The final period or comma goes inside the quoted material. "Yay!"
  • Semi-colons separate two independent clauses. "I am a writer; I am a reader." Refined folk use semi-colons for style points; in all cases, semi-colons could be periods if the clauses can stand alone.

  • Pronoun Power!
    /\/
    Pronoun
    A pronoun should refer a noun coming before the pronoun.
    Example: Mary is late for work. She is now sweaty.
    (Him/Her)
    Possessive Pronoun
    Example: Mary is late for work because she cannot find her keys.
    (His/Her)
    Antecedent (an·tuh·see·dnt)
    The noun being replaced by the pronoun. In the examples above, it'd be "Mary."
  • References to other work
    Movies/Books
    The basic format for an in-text citation is: Title of the Book (Author Last Name, year). One author: Where the Wild Things Are (Sendak, 1963) is a depiction of a child coping with his anger towards his mom.
  • Confusing Words
    Affect/Effect
    Affect is a verb meaning to influence. The effect is a noun meaning result. Example: The bird shriek affected my concentration. The effect (impact) of the shriek was distraction.
