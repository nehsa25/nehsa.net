Pick Indeed!
October 6th, 2024
Jesse Stone

I was stagnating at my current employer, which I had been with for seven years, and needed a fresh start. I signed up for a paid LinkedIn subscription and began exploring the new opportunities available.

A few months later, after discovering an exciting new opportunity, I reflected on the experience. What immediately comes to mind: LinkedIn sucks.

LinkedIn did not produce interviews. The problems with LinkedIn were two-fold:

  1. It's too big. It's too popular. TOO MANY PEOPLE.
  2. The "EASY" submit feature was too easy. It allows for too many applications, too quickly. A new job posting might get hundreds of applications within minutes of the post.

None of the "premium" services offers such as the ability to indicate a job is a "Top Choice" offset these systemic issues.

At the same time I paid for a subscription at LinkedIn, I also updated my resume on a free version of Indeed. This generated every fruitful conversation in the end.

Indeed, it is the clear winner in my head. The day I officially accepted my offer from the new employer, I canceled my paid subscription to LinkedIn and have no plans to return.
